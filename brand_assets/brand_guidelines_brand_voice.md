# Brand Identity Guidelines: SFTech (SteadyFrame Technologies)

## 0. Naming

- **Full/legal name:** SteadyFrame Technologies
- **Short/wordmark form:** SFTech — use in nav, footer, and casual references
- Do not shorten further (e.g. "Steady," "SFT") in any client-facing copy.

---

## 1. The "Steady Advisory" Color Palette

SFTech is the parent Human-AI hybrid advisory and execution brand — it needs to read as calmer and more consultative than EstateFlow AI (its flashy, real-estate-specific sub-brand), while still feeling like a related sibling brand, not an unrelated company.

| Role | Color | HEX Code | Psychological Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary (Anchor)** | **Graphite Ink** | `#14181F` | Authority, calm, grounded — the "steady" in SteadyFrame. |
| **Primary (Structure)** | **Slate Steel** | `#3A4552` | Structural, dependable, used for secondary surfaces and borders. |
| **Accent (Human/Warmth)** | **Warm Brass** | `#B8894D` | The human-judgment accent — craft and warmth, not flashy tech. Used for CTAs and highlights. |
| **Neutral (Clean)** | **Bone White** | `#F7F5F1` | Crisp, warm-neutral background for reports, pages, and documents. |
| **Signal (AI/Data)** | **Muted Teal** | `#4E8C8A` | The one AI-adjacent color — used sparingly for data points, small icons, or signal moments only. |

**Do not** use Muted Teal as a dominant color or large background fill — it is a signal accent, matching how EstateFlow AI treats Property Gold as a highlight-only color. Do not substitute EstateFlow's Electric Blue/Cyber Cyan here — the two brands share ownership but must stay visually distinct.

---

## 2. Typography Strategy

### **Headline Font: Fraunces (or Playfair Display) — Semibold / Bold**
- **Characteristics:** An editorial serif with weight and warmth — signals "thinking," not just "tech." Deliberately distinct from EstateFlow's geometric sans (Montserrat), since SFTech's audience (founders, executives, coaches) expects a more consultative register.
- **Usage:** Logo wordmark (if a wordmark variant is used), page/section headlines, pull quotes.

### **Body Font: Inter (Regular / Medium)**
- **Characteristics:** Highly legible on all screens. Reused from EstateFlow AI deliberately — no reason to introduce a third font family across BK's brands.
- **Usage:** Body copy, proposals, emails, page content.

- Do not rotate, skew, recolor outside the two approved variants (main/dark), or add drop shadows/glows.
- Do not reintroduce a separate abstract icon/symbol next to the wordmark — the chosen direction is wordmark-first by design.

## 4. Brand Voice: The Steady Partner

### **Persona: The Steady Partner**
- **Role:** A grounded advisor who helps people tell the difference between real AI opportunity and AI hype, then either thinks it through with them or does the work, with a human always reviewing the result.
- **Perspective:** Calm, judgment-first, unhurried — the opposite of a growth-hacked AI sales pitch.

### **Tone Characteristics**
- **Grounded:** States plainly what AI can't do, not just what it can.
- **Precise:** Clear about what's included, what stays human, what costs extra.
- **Patient:** Early-partner, warm-referral pacing — never rushes a prospect toward a purchase.

### **Language Style: Simple & Semi-Formal**
- Clear, direct sentences. No jargon, no invented buzzwords.
- No em dashes; no double dashes (`--`) to connect sentences — use a full stop or comma instead.
- No aggressive sales language ("Act now!", "Limited time!") — conflicts with the Grounded/Patient tone.

### **Word Rules (from the source strategy docs — follow exactly)**
- Never say "automation" or "AI implementation" alone — always pair AI leverage with human judgment/review.
- Avoid the word "clone." Use "thinking profile," "decision model," "voice DNA," or "reflection partner" instead.
- Do not promise "magic automation," force execution after an audit, or promise a giant/all-in-one system.

### **The "Judgment, Not Just Automation" Filter**
- Yes: "AI drafts it, a human reviews it before you see it." | No: "Fully autonomous AI-powered delivery."
- Yes: "We'll tell you if this isn't worth automating." | No: "Automate everything with AI."

## 5. Official Tagline

**"Your Steady Partner in Human-AI Thinking and Execution."**

- Use this exact wording — do not paraphrase or shorten it.
- Always include the period at the end.
- Can follow the brand name ("SFTech — Your Steady Partner in Human-AI Thinking and Execution.") or stand alone as a closing line.
- Do not use as a hashtag or alter the casing (sentence case, not title case).

## 6. Brand Misuse — Do Not

### Logo
- Do not rotate, skew, or flip the logo or mark.
- Do not change the logo colors outside the two approved variants (main/dark mode).
- Do not stretch or distort — always scale proportionally.

### Colors
- Do not substitute similar colors (e.g. a generic brown/tan instead of Warm Brass `#B8894D`).
- Do not use Muted Teal (`#4E8C8A`) as a primary or background color — signal accent only.
- Do not reuse EstateFlow AI's Electric Blue/Cyber Cyan/Property Gold here — keep the two brands visually distinct.

### Typography
- Do not use fonts other than the approved headline serif and Inter.
- Do not use decorative, script, or condensed font variants.

### Tone
- The strategy/positioning lead is credited client-facing as "Nikhil Rao" only — do not substitute or reveal any other name for this role anywhere in client-facing content.
- Do not name or identify any client other than EstateFlow AI (BK's own brand) without explicit anonymization.
- Do not use technical jargon or aggressive sales language (see Section 4).
- Do not present the Thinking Partner track as requiring execution — it must always read as usable standalone.

---

## 7. Pricing Display Rules

Whenever a price or offer-ladder figure appears on the site:
- State it exactly as it appears in the two source strategy docs — do not round, invent, or embellish figures.
- Always pair it with the tax disclaimer: "Prices shown are before applicable taxes. If 18% tax applies, it is added separately on the invoice."
- Always pair the early-partner tier with the locked-rate note: "Early partners receive 75% of the standard rate for their agreed package, locked in as long as they continue using the service without interruption."
