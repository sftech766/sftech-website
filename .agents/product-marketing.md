# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-07-11*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** SFTech (SteadyFrame Technologies) helps thoughtful people and small teams find where AI is actually worth using in their work, then either helps them think it through or does the work for them, with a human reviewing everything.

**What it does:** SFTech runs a Human-AI hybrid service across two tracks. Track One, AI Thinking Partner, is a strategy/audit layer that identifies which workflows are worth improving with AI and which should stay human, without requiring the client to buy execution. Track Two, Done-For-You Human-AI Workflow Services, is the execution layer — AI does the first draft, a human handles context, judgment, and quality control, and the client gets a finished, usable result instead of a 70-80%-done AI output they still have to fix themselves.

**Product category:** Human-AI hybrid consulting and workflow execution service (not a SaaS tool, not a pure automation agency).

**Product type:** Service — advisory (Track One) plus done-for-you delivery (Track Two).

**Business model:** Free diagnostic call as entry point, then a paid offer ladder per track (see Proof Points/pricing below). Early partners get 75% of standard rate, locked for that package as long as they keep using the service without interruption. All prices before applicable taxes (18% added separately on invoice).

## Target Audience

**Track One (AI Thinking Partner) — target companies/individuals:** Founders, creators, coaches, executive/leadership coaches, consultants, authors, speakers, newsletter writers, community builders, course creators, solopreneurs — people with many recurring tasks or decisions who don't yet know what's worth automating or AI-assisting.

**Track Two (Human-AI Workflow Services) — target companies/individuals:** Small business owners and service professionals with repeated workflow pain — real estate agents/realtors, CPAs/tax consultants, insurance agents, mortgage brokers, immigration consultants, clinic owners, local service business owners, workshop/webinar hosts, business/marketing consultants, financial advisors, course creators.

**Decision-makers:** Almost always the individual owner/operator/creator themselves — these are small teams or solo operators, not committees.

**Primary use case:** "I know AI could probably help somewhere in my work, but I don't know where to start, what's worth doing, or who does the last 20% of polish so it's actually usable."

**Jobs to be done:**
- Tell me which of my repeated tasks are actually worth improving with AI (and which aren't).
- Do the AI-plus-human work for me so I get a finished, client-ready result, not a rough draft I still have to fix.
- Let me test this cheaply and low-risk before committing to a bigger system.

**Use cases (drawn directly from the two source docs):**
- AI Workflow Audit / AI Opportunity Map / Workflow ROI Review / Automation Readiness Review / Implementation Thinking Partner (Track One outputs).
- Podcast/long-form content repurposed into clips, quotes, captions, titles.
- Social media structure, content pillars, weekly calendar, drafted posts.
- Research briefs on mentors, clients, partners, prospects.
- Website funnel and GEO improvement (evolving a site from SEO-only to AI-search/answer-engine-aware).
- Post/content performance review, voice-aligned content suggestions.
- Free giveaways/funnel assets built from existing worksheets, frameworks, talks.
- Client/audience research; recurring admin, research, or content support.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** AI can get a task 70-80% done, but the last 20% (matching voice, correcting details, judgment calls, packaging, iterating) takes hours, is frustrating, and often ends with "maybe I should have just done this myself."

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic AI tools produce a draft but no judgment about whether the workflow was worth automating in the first place.
- Full automation/AI agencies sell implementation regardless of fit, creating a bias toward "automate everything."
- DIY AI use burns the client's own time on the last-20% problem instead of removing it.

**What it costs them:** Hours of manual cleanup per AI-assisted task, inconsistent quality, and either abandoned AI experiments or half-used tools.

**Emotional tension:** Frustration that a "close but not exact" AI output isn't actually saving time; hesitation about whether AI is hype or genuinely useful for their specific work.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** Generic AI automation/consulting agencies — fall short because they sell automation as the default answer and don't do the audit-first, "some things should stay human" work.

**Secondary:** DIY use of ChatGPT/Claude/other AI tools directly — falls short because the client absorbs the entire last-20% burden themselves (context, voice-matching, correction, packaging).

**Indirect:** Hiring a full-time employee, VA, or freelancer for the same recurring work — falls short on cost and ramp-up time for small, specific, often part-time workflow needs.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Explicit two-door structure: strategy/audit (Track One) is decoupled from execution (Track Two) — a Track One client is never pressured into buying delivery.
- Human-AI hybrid delivery model: AI for speed/leverage, human for context, judgment, quality control, and final packaging.
- Warm-referral-led, trust-first growth model (not cold volume outreach) — signals a boutique, relationship-based service rather than a scaled agency.
- Early-partner pricing with a locked rate, rewarding first movers with real skin in the game (feedback, testimonials, introductions).

**How we do it differently:** Start with a free diagnostic to sort "should this stay human," "should this be AI-assisted," and "should this be automated," rather than assuming everything should be automated.

**Why that's better:** Clients get the right-sized answer for their specific workflow, not a one-size-fits-all automation pitch.

**Why customers choose us:** They get a finished, usable result without having to become the AI-tooling expert themselves, and they can start small (a single research brief or a 30-60 minute call) before trusting us with anything larger.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Isn't this just reselling ChatGPT output?" | No — pricing explicitly includes human interview/discovery, workflow review, prioritization by ROI/risk, and human quality control/editing/packaging, not just an AI-generated answer. |
| "How do I know this isn't oversold automation hype?" | The Thinking Partner track is explicitly decoupled from execution — the audit doesn't force a sale, and the "What We Will Not Promise" list rules out magic automation, forced execution, and giant systems. |
| "Why should I pay before knowing if it'll help?" | Entry point is a free 30-60 minute diagnostic call with no cost and no commitment; paid work only starts after a specific opportunity is identified. |

**Anti-persona:** AI-native founders, AI agency owners, AI automation consultants, VC-backed AI SaaS founders, and enterprise AI-transformation buyers — not the early target; these audiences already have their own AI point of view and aren't the "workflow pain, not AI hype" ICP this service is built for.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Frustration with AI tools that get 70-80% done and leave a time-consuming, quality-uncertain last 20% to clean up themselves.

**Pull:** A free, no-pressure diagnostic call plus a track (Thinking Partner) that never forces an execution purchase — low-risk way to test whether this is useful.

**Habit:** Continuing to muddle through AI tools solo, or continuing to pay a generalist VA/freelancer for work that's really an AI-plus-review job.

**Anxiety:** Worry that "AI service" means a canned automation pitch, low-quality output, or losing control over their voice/judgment in the work.
